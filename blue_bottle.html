Blue Bottle

Introduction

The “blue bottle” reaction is a classic chemistry demonstration. It is often used in general science classes to introduce the roles of observation and hypothesis in the scientific method. The demonstration is used in chemistry classes to illustrate oxidation and reduction reactions, and also to study the rates of chemical reactions.

Materials

8g Dextrose

1% Methylene Blue

8g Potassium Hydroxide

Water

500mL Flask

Rubber Stopper

500mL Graduated Cylinder

2 Weighing Dishes

Procedure

1. To prepare the “blue bottle” solution, add 8 g of potassium hydroxide to 300 mL of water in a 500-mL flask. Stir until the solid is dissolved. Add 10 g of dextrose and a few drops of methylene blue indicator solution. Fill to the 500-mL mark, stopper or cap the flask, and mix thoroughly.

2. Allow the “blue bottle” solution to stand undisturbed in the stoppered flask until the solution is colorless. This may take a few minutes.

3. Show students the colorless solution, then gently shake the flask to obtain the blue color.

4. Wait patiently as the solution turns colorless again.

5. Repeat steps 2 and 3 and ask students to record observations and propose possible tests to explain the colorless– blue–colorless sequence of color changes. The process can be repeated several times over a 10–15 minute period. Periodically remove the stopper to introduce more air (oxygen) into the reaction flask.
