Elephant's Toothpaste

Introduction

Mix together hydrogen peroxide, sodium iodide solution, and dishwashing liquid in a tall cylinder and stand back. Your students will observe with amazement a catalyst in action as an enormous amount of soapy foam erupts from “Old Foamey!”

Materials

20mL Hydrogen Peroxide

5mL Sodium Iodide

10mL Dish Soap

Food Coloring

10mL Graduated Cylinder

100mL Graduated Cylinder

Procedure

1. Measure out 20 mL of the 30% hydrogen peroxide into the 100-mL graduated cylinder.

2. Measure out 10 mL of dishwashing liquid into the 10-mL graduated cylinder and add it to the cylinder containing the hydrogen peroxide. Add a few drops of food coloring, if desired.

3. Measure out 5 mL of sodium iodide solution using the 10-mL graduated cylinder. Quickly but carefully, add the sodium iodide solution to the 100-mL graduated cylinder.

4. Step back and observe the reaction.
